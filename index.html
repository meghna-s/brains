WHAT IS ALS?


abbr.
amyotrophic lateral sclerosis

ALS is a neurological disease that weakens the body's neurons. Also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, it destroys the body's ability to control voluntary muscles and eventually causes the death of motor neurons.
Those affected by ALS may suffer from muscle weakness, speech problems, respiratory failure, or a variety of other symptoms. A fatal neurological disease, ALS has no known cause or common diagnosis, although researchers have found genetic mutations associated with some cases.
